Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

My visit to Kuala Lumpur took place during my high school days, when my family and I resided in Singapore. My family and I made a couple of trips down to Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, as the city was merely an hour away from Singapore through a flight. Kuala Lumpur, the capital of Malaysia, is a city bustling with residents and tourists. Kuala Lumpur has a hot and humid weather for most of the year, and wittnesses heavy rains during the monsoon season. The monsoon season heightens the beauty of Kuala Lumpur's scenic natural environment. Unlike a lot of metrapolitan cities, Kuala Lumpur is filled with nature, with road and pathways decoarated with trees and flowers that bloom during the monsoon season. KL is also home to multiple tourist attractions, some of which I stopped by durig my visits such as the Petronas twin towers, KL bird park, Little India, and Batu Caves.



Places to Visit in Kuala Lumpur

Petronas Twin Towers

The Petronas Towers, also known as the Petronas Twin Towers or KLCC Twin Towers, are a pair of 88-storey supertall skyscrapers in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, standing at 451.9 metres. The Petronas Towers has multiple attractions associated to it, such as the KLCC park, the Sky Bridge, and the retail center located inside the building. The Twin Towers have featured in multiple shows and movies!

Kuala Lumpur Tower

Kuala Lumpur Tower, also known as KL Tower and is a 6-story, 421-meter-tall telecommunication tower in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. It is the world's 7th tallest tower, also, Malaysia and Southeast Asia's 2nd tallest tower. Tourists visit KL Tower to have a 360-degree view of the city. The lift takes only 54 seconds going up to the observation deck and takes 52 seconds to come down.

Kuala Lumpur Bird Park

Kuala Lumpur Bird Park public aviary in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. The Bird Park houses more than 3,000 birds representing more than 200 species in an enclosed aviary. About 90% are local birds and 10% were imported from fprien countries. The gardens include an artificial lake, the National Monument, the Kuala Lumpur Butterfly Park, the Deer Park, Orchid and Hibiscus gardens, and the former Malaysia Parliament House.
