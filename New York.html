New York City, New York

My first visit to New York was fairly recent, in August of 2020. My older sister, who migrated to the United States in 2016, resides in New Jersey. As New Jersey is fairly close to New York, we took it upon ourselves to make a visit to the city. This trip was an enjoyable one, as my sister and I explored the streets of New York from cafes to state parks to restaurants, to the campus of NYU! New York was the embodiment of a bustling metrapolitan, with busy streets filled people, cars, and shops. My favourite memory from this trip is visiting the Harry Potter Store in the city, and purchasing Harry Potter merch! Another exciting event was attending the Broadway enactment of Aladdin!



Places to Visit in New York City

Central Park

Central Park is a popular New York City destination, and is filled with nature and scenic natural phenomenon. During the winter, Central Park is a popular destination for ice-skating across the Wollman Rink. Central Park also has multiple tourist attractions such as Belvedere Castle, Central Park Zoo, Bethesda Fountain and Terrace, and more. It is the fifth-largest park in New York City, covering 843 acres.

Times Square

Times Square, often featured in movies and shows, is lined with brilliantly lit billboards and screens, and is the place to go in New York during the evening or during the day. Times Square is one of the busiest and most bustling locations in New York City, with perpetual crowd and activity. Times Square was named in 1904 after the New York Times tower. Times Square is also the location of the Madame Tussaud's Wax Museum, the home of life-like figurines of NYC celebrities.

Brooklyn Bridge

The Brooklyn Bridge is a hybrid cable-stayed/suspension bridge in New York City, spanning the East River between the boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn. Opened on May 24, 1883, the Brooklyn Bridge was the first fixed crossing of the East River. From the bridge are beautiful views over Manhattan, the East River, and beyond to the Statue of Liberty. A wood plank walkway, only open to pedestrians and cyclists, runs above the lanes of traffic.
